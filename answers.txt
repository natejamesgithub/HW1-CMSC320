Q3: What is the difference between Merging and Rebasing?
Answer: 
Merging combines commit histories into a new "merge commit," 
preserving the historical context of divergent work. 
Rebasing, on the other hand, rewrites commit history by 
reapplying your changes on top of a different base branch, 
creating a linear history without merge commits.
